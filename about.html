About this webring.

A global ring connecting competitive debaters and the things they make.

Why debate?

Competitive debate produces a specific kind of person: someone who has spent a lot of time thinking carefully about ideas, building arguments, and listening to the other side. Debaters come from everywhere — Nairobi, Oslo, São Paulo, Taipei, everywhere — competing in British Parliamentary, World Schools, policy, Lincoln-Douglas, and every other format.

A lot of them create things. Blogs, newsletters, YouTube channels, podcasts. Writing about rounds, about theory, about the ideas that come up in debate and stay with them afterward. But they're scattered. Hard to find unless you already know where to look. This ring exists to make the community visible — to connect people across formats, circuits, and countries, and make it a little easier to stumble into something worth reading.

What makes this one different?

Most webrings require a GitHub pull request to join and only accept traditional websites. This one uses a form and accepts any platform — websites, Substacks, YouTube channels, podcasts, newsletters. If you don't write code, a pull request is a real barrier. Most people in this ring aren't here because they code.

If you make a weekly newsletter about debate theory, or run a YouTube channel breaking down rounds, that belongs here just as much as a personal website. The format doesn't matter. The content does.
